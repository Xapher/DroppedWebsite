June 7, 2016

dgainey : June 8, 2016 3:50 am : Pastor's Blog

In case you missed our last newsletter, Dustin DeJong is our new youth and children’s director! He begins this Sunday. Dustin’s married to Lori and they’re parents to Peyton (7) and Colby (6) and expect another boy in late August. Both are long time Tucsonans who’re excited to call the Oasis home. I’m thrilled to have the DeJongs join us and to have Dustin partner with me in serving our gracious congregation!

Hope and I decided to split up our vacation this summer so I could prepare for taking the youth to Fuge (Dustin will join us). We’ll take off a couple of weeks later this summer, but we just returned from a short trip to Florida to celebrate my parents’ 50-year wedding anniversary. We were joined by my parents, my brother and his family, and my sister and her family.

It probably comes as no surprise that the highlight of the trip involved saltwater fishing. I took the girls inshore fishing twice. I paid for the first one, but since we caught nothing but catfish, the guide took us the following morning without charging us. Captain Clint was kind, patient and thoughtful. He took time to teach the girls to fish and never raised his voice. He also threw the cast-net like a pro.

We didn’t catch much, but we had a blast on the water. We saw dolphin, sharks, starfish, conch shells, barracuda and a puffer fish. We even fished from sandbars in the middle of the bay. There’s something about being on a boat that makes me come alive. Catching a couple of fish didn’t hurt, either. I took a few moments on the boat to thank God for the beauty around me and the opportunity to do something I deeply love. God is kind.

The second fishing trip was followed by the most difficult part of the trip—family conflict. It was ugly. Worse still, it didn’t get resolved by bedtime. So, the next day I spoke with my siblings and parents separately. My longing was to do everything in my power to resolve the conflict before it turned into irreparable bitterness. I followed the admonition of Paul in Romans 12.18: If it is possible, as far as it depends on you, live at peace with everyone.

I asked forgiveness from some and confronted others with how they hurt me or others, offering forgiveness to those who wounded me. I found myself submitting to the very sermon I had recorded for Memorial Day weekend. It wasn’t pleasant, but it was good. Friendship can only exist between people who’re committed to do the hard stuff necessary for reconciliation. So, the trip was good—very good—but I wouldn’t exactly call it a vacation :-)

On Sunday, we continued our series in Luke with 4:14-30. If you missed the message, you can listen to it here.

Luke gives us a glimpse into the kind of message Jesus preached in the synagogues of Galilee and Judea. After reading from Isaiah, Jesus sat down, stared at the congregation, and announced that this reading had been fulfilled in their hearing. Jesus declared Jubilee.

Jubilee was supposed to be celebrated once every 49 or 50 years. Jewish slaves would be set free, debts would be forgiven, property would be returned, and the land would lie fallow—only Isael didn’t recognized the year of Jubilee because it was too radical.

Then Jesus came along and declared that Jubilee had begun! The freedom and joy their ancestors had longed for was finally here. Jesus, the Son of God, would set the captives free, open the eyes of the blind, and preach good news to the poor. Because of his unique identity, he would not only proclaim Jubilee, Jesus would make it happen.

This message fell on deaf ears. The people of Nazareth were impressed by their favorite son, but they weren’t moved by his words. Jesus’ message didn’t seem to apply to them. They didn’t view themselves as poor, needy, blind, or imprisoned. Jesus’ words were nice. They were bold, but they didn’t apply.

So Jesus told them he would go to those who recognized their need. He would go to the desperate. Jesus would go to Gentiles. When he said this, his hometown elders took him outside and tried to throw him off a cliff. Wow! Things went sideways quickly.

The same is true for us. We have good news for those who recognize their need. When we share it, some will reject our message and others will welcome it, just as they did with Jesus. The former will break our hearts, but the latter will make it all worthwhile when they join our forever family and enter the Kingdom of God. So keep sharing it!

Jesus’ genealogy tells us that he came to rescue us all. If both of these statements are true, the world needs to know. That’s why we share our faith and support missionaries.
